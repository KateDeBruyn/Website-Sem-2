About

Welcome to a site created by Keightyrose dedicated to data visualisation, representation and art.

Data visualisation entails the act of visually representing types and forms of raw data to produce information that users can engage and interact with. This site bases not only its blogs, visualisation methods, and artwork, but its entire constuction too on research in the data visualisation field. For more information, see below.

The What and The Why

Over the course of the year, we have been learning not only how to create our own unique websites, but about how these websites are developed with aesthetic and representational strategies in mind. This website in particular draws from academic readings, essays, papers and research on UI and UX design, Data Visualisation, Visualisation and Interaction research, and Data-Driven Art research. The website has been built off literature in these fields by authors such as Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic, Evanthia Dimara and Charles Perin, and Fernanda B. Vi√©gas and Martin Wattenberg to name a few. These authors feature across the website and have been extensively noted for their contributions to the making of this site.

The aesthetic stategies used across the site have been formulated by the UX and UX design and the necessary research. Representation throughout the site involves the visualisation of data - the science and practice of visually representing data as consumable, understandable information This website makes use of types of bar graphs to represent gaming data and information. The aesthetics and representation strategies are also based around each other. For example, the site is structured in grids which mimic a similar construction to bar graphs. The aim of this was to use complementary aesthetic and representational strategies to bring you a cohesive and comprehensive website.
